A page pressed between dried leaves and bound with a silken thread. The ink shimmers faintly when touched. The hand that wrote it is steady, though the letters seem older than the page.

I do not mark time as others do. There was a beginning, once. A veil. A clearing. A question.

The spirit came to me when the stars were still. Childlike in shape, but older than memory. It did not speak. It didn’t need to.

It watched me kneel. It watched the dew form around my robes. And then the clearing… breathed.

I do not remember parting the veil. Only the feeling of it parting me.

Visions passed like light through water. Unknowable. Unwritten. I tried to hold them, but they passed through my palms like wind. Not to be kept — only witnessed.

When it was over, I remained. The spirit was gone, but not lost. Its echo lingers in the trees — not as voice, but as shimmer. A reminder that I am no longer waiting.

The others may seek answers. Closure. Rest. I carry only silence now. And in silence, I understand more than ever.

The veil is gone.

The path is not.

I walk it still.
