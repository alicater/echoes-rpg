A page tucked between loose stones in a forgotten wall. No name. Just a faint fox sigil drawn in the corner, glowing briefly before fading.

They always say the Vale is cursed. I never cared for curses. But secrets? Secrets are worth the risk.

The crypt wasn’t on any map. That’s how I knew it was real.

Stone slick with moss. Tombs cracked open like clams. And there, near the back — a chest. Locked, of course. Whispering, almost. The kind of lock that remembers the last hands that touched it.

It opened too easily.

Inside, a crystal pulsing soft purple. Warm, like breath. When I lifted it, it spoke. Not in words — in memory. I saw shadows moving. Mine. Others. Something clever in me stirred. Something afraid, too.

Behind me, something shifted on the wall. A shape — not quite a person. Not quite gone. I didn’t turn around.

Instead, I laughed. Quietly. I always knew I wasn’t alone down here.

The sigil on the floor — a fox, etched cleaner than the rest — began to glow. I touched it once, and it vanished. Not a warning. A signature.

I left the way I came. No relics. No treasure. Just the knowledge that someone had been waiting for me to find them. And that I did.

Let them echo.

Let them wonder.

The trail’s already gone cold.
