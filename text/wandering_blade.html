A torn entry, found in the field journal of an unnamed soldier. Handwriting steady. The ink hesitates.

No more orders. No more drills. No more names to remember maps to burn.

I came because I couldn’t stop hearing them. Boots. Steel. Screams. Voices that belonged to men who didn’t come back. My name among them.

Hollow Vale did not judge me. The fog thickened, and they were there — Bleeding. Broken. Standing. Silent. Watching.

I found the altar. The one from the dreams. A slab of stone where a sword should end. I placed mine there. Buried it. Let it rest. It felt like an exhale I had forgotten how to take.

The veil moved. I think… I think they nodded.

I don’t deserve peace. I walked forward anyway.

The sword stayed. The ghosts did not follow. My back didn’t ache when I stood up. That scared me more than anything.

Let this be the last page. Let me forget. Let me be something more than the blade.

— R.
